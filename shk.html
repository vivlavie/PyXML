Functionality

3.1 There shall be a collision monitoring system able to detect a vessel on collision course. The system shall present vessel movements on graphics that are based on marine charts. The system shall provide the facility to track and identify the vessels, initiate alarms should the vessels enter pre-defined exclusion zones or be on course that would in any way present a risk to the installation or the operation in the proximity of the platform. The information from the tracking system may be integrated with data from other installations and made available to other agencies. As such the system shall have the capability to store and hand off the data. 3.2 To register the course and speed of the object The radar system shall be able to register the course and speed of the object, and plotting facilities shall be included, e.g. automatic radar, plotting aid. Radar signals shall be transmitted to unit responsible for surveillance. The RADAR system shall be interfaced with 'StatoilHydro Surveillance RADAR system' (Statoil Marine VTS) 3.3 To provide exact location of RUBY FPSO for vessels to avoid collision course A Differential Global Positioning System (DGPS) shall provide accurate positional information to various systems 3.4 To provide interfaces between RADAR and other systems to provide trajectory predictions and collision avoidance alerts for safety marine operation. Two (2) complete sets of radar shall be provided, with long range (S band) and accuracy (X band) features fitted with automatic radar plotting aids (ARPA) and interfaced with Gyrocompass, Automatic Identification system (AIS), DGPS systems to provide trajectory predictions and collision avoidance alerts for safety marine operation.
- The radars shall display in the radio room and Offloading Control Room
- The radar shall give all round coverage around the FPSO with minimum blind sector allowed by classification rules, and with a detection distance of 30 nautical Miles from the FPSO.

- Radars components shall comply with Electromagnetic Compatibility Regulations

Helideck

None Helideck To provide a safe landing and take off area for helicopter operation Helideck structure Access Route to the Helideck

Functionality

5.1 Helideck structure shall be capable, for the duration of the operational life of the platform, of providing structural support that accommodates the relevant helicopter operations ensuring stability under all foreseeable operational, environmental conditions. Super Puma AS332L2
The Helideck strength shall comply with CAP 437, CAAP 92-2 and AMSA Marine orders parts 12,14,17 and 60 and shall be suitable for the largest helicopter anticipated to be used at the RUBY FPSO. 5.2 To withstand impact load The Helideck shall be capable of withstanding heavy normal landing and emergency landing as specified in CAP 437, based on the largest helicopter anticipated to be used at the RUBY FPSO. 5.3 To provide three (3) access points Three(3) access points(120 degrees between each access point) shall be arranged to escape upwind and removable or collapsible handrail will be arranged where exceeding the height limitation. 5.4 To provide navigational aids Flush type tie-down point shall be provided on helideck surface of landing area and parking area. Drain pipe shall be fitted to each tie down point and shall be connected to gutter drain line.
One (1) set of illuminated windsock and supporting post shall be provided. Supporting post shall be provided with means of access for maintenance of windsock and light.
Glide path indicator(GPI)/Helicopter approach path indicator(HAPI) for day and night medevac shall be provided

Availability

5.1 Hull None Helideck design shall minimise the potential environmental effects (turbulence air movement, thermal effects, flammable gas), as far as practicable. Operations restrictions shall be clearly specified in the Helicopter Operations Procedures. 5.2 Helideck structure None Unserviceable helicopter shall be handled by the pedestal crane to portside laydown area or offshore service vessel.

None Ballast and bilge system - based on Goliat FPSO The ballast water system shall provide easy and reliable facilities for ballast water distribution in order to maintain control of the floating offshore installation during routine operations and emergency situations, in terms of stability, heel, trim and draft and ensuring that hull stresses do not exceed the design strength criteria.  
The bilge system shall provide easy and reliable facilities for pumping from and draining watertight compartments. Pump in E/R)

Functionality

6.1 To maintain heel, trim and hull girder loads within normal operating limits and to
prevent severe weather bow emergence and re-entry slamming at light drafts without recourse to emergency ballasting of cargo or slops tanks The capacity of the ballast system shall also comply with §11 of the NMD Ballast regulations. 6.2 To maintain ballasting in the event of failure of one pump The ballast system shall be equipped with at least two independent pumps. 6.3 To cross-feed ballast water from one side of the vessel to the other The ballast system shall be arranged with cross connections ensuring that ballast water can be cross fed from one side of the vessel to the other as a contingency measure. 6.4 To provide fail-safe function for the ballast valves Ballast system valves shall be designed fail-to-close 6.5 To drain watertight compartments An effective bilge system shall be provided for draining watertight compartments, e.g. primary bilge pump and emergency bilge pump.

Availability
